Our Journey in Helsinki

Alhamdulillah, Masjid Sunnah (مسجد أهل السنّة والجماعة) was established to serve the growing Muslim population in Helsinki, providing a place of worship centrally located in Ylä-Malmi.

What began as a small gathering of families seeking to establish the daily prayers has blossomed into a full-service Islamic center. We operate firmly upon the Quran and the Sunnah, according to the understanding of the righteous predecessors (Ahl Al-Sunnah Wal-Jama`ah).

Our goal is to preserve the Islamic identity of our families while being positive, contributing members to the wider Finnish society. We emphasize authentic Islamic education, community welfare, and pure monotheism (Tawheed).

Mission & Vision

Our Mission: To provide a welcoming, educational, and spiritual environment for Muslims in Helsinki, anchored in the authentic teachings of Islam, while fostering strong community bonds.

Our Vision: To be a model Islamic center in Scandinavia, producing a generation of Muslims who are steadfast in their faith, educated in their religion, and exemplary in their character.

Faith (Iman)

Building our lives around Tawheed and the Sunnah.

Service (Khidmah)

Supporting the needy and aiding our neighbors.

Education (Ilm)

Pursuing authentic knowledge from reliable scholars.

Unity (Ukhuwwah)

Fostering brotherhood and sisterhood across cultures.

Our Facilities

Located at Ylä-Malmin tori 6b, our center is equipped to serve the community.

  • Men's Prayer Hall (Capacity: 300)
  • Dedicated Sisters' Section
  • Modern Wudu Facilities
  • 3 Classrooms
  • Islamic Library
  • Nearby Parking Available

Our Milestone History

Establishment

The initial gathering of families mapping out the need for a dedicated Masjid upon the Sunnah in Helsinki.

Moving to Ylä-Malmi

Securing the lease at Ylä-Malmin tori 6b and completing the initial renovations for the prayer halls.

Launching Educational Programs

Starting the weekend Quran school and the regular weekly adult halaqat.

Open Doors Policy

Masjid Sunnah welcomes visitors of all backgrounds. If you are a student, researcher, neighbor, or just curious about Islam, you are welcome to visit our center, observe the prayers, and ask questions.
